About Eagle Harbor

Discover the rich history and heritage of our treasured waterfront community

Our Heritage

A Place of Freedom, Beauty, and Opportunity

Eagle Harbor is a rare and treasured community along the Patuxent Riverâ€”one of a few historic waterfront African American communities remaining in the United States. Our town was founded in 1929 as a place of freedom, beauty, and opportunity during a time when such spaces were precious and few.

Here, the rush of city life gives way to peace, natural splendor, and a sense of belonging. From the sunrise over the Patuxent River to eagles soaring above, Eagle Harbor offers a daily escape and serves as a reminder of life's simple joys.

We are committed to preserving our heritage while providing a welcoming space for residents and visitors to relax, connect, and enjoy recreational activities in our waterfront environment.

Our Timeline

1929
Foundation of Eagle Harbor

Eagle Harbor was established as one of the first African American waterfront resort communities in Maryland, providing a safe haven for families seeking recreation and community.

1950s-60s
Golden Era of Growth

The community flourished as a popular destination for African American families from Washington D.C. and Baltimore, featuring beaches, cottages, and recreational facilities.

2018
Sustainable Community Designation

Maryland designated Eagle Harbor as a Sustainable Community, recognizing our commitment to environmental stewardship and community resilience.

2020
National Heritage Recognition

Destination Southern Maryland National Heritage Area designated Eagle Harbor as a Historical Black Site, cementing our place in American history.

2025
New Leadership Era

Mayor James S. Jones and new commissioners were sworn in, bringing fresh vision while honoring our rich heritage and community traditions.

What Makes Us Special

Historic Significance

As one of the few remaining historic African American waterfront communities in the United States, we preserve an important piece of American history and culture.

Environmental Stewardship

Our Sustainable Community designation reflects our commitment to environmental protection, climate resilience, and responsible waterfront development.

Patuxent River Access

Direct access to the beautiful Patuxent River provides opportunities for boating, fishing, kayaking, and enjoying Maryland's natural waterways.

Close-Knit Community

With about 70 residents, Eagle Harbor maintains the intimate, family-friendly atmosphere that has defined our community for nearly a century.

Strategic Location

Located in Prince George's County, we offer peaceful waterfront living while remaining accessible to Washington D.C. and Baltimore metropolitan areas.

Active Community Life

Regular events, town meetings, and celebrations keep our community connected and engaged throughout the year.
